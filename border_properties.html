Generator Z

But a new generation is on the rise and the first step to communicating with them, is understanding they aren't just another Millennial. ― Pamela La Gioia

With the Teen Opinions Count survey, 10,000 young people in Southeast Michigan and Western New York’s cities, suburbs, towns, and rural communities weighed in on what matters to them.

The Generator Z website unfurled over multiple release dates. To lay the groundwork for the initiative’s reason for being, we built data visualizations that tell the story behind the numbers in the Teen Opinions Count survey.

The findings of this survey served as the raw ingredients that became Generator Z.

Equipped with thousands and thousands of data points from Teen Opinions Count, the foundation teased out ten themes that surfaced again and again across demographic groups.

Today’s teens are driven by incredible curiosity; have an awe-inspiring command of technology; see society through serious awareness of themselves and the world around them; and are ready to lead in times of change
